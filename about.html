What is Fantastical Art?

According to Wikipedia, "Fantastic art is a broad and loosely defined art genre. It is not restricted to a specific school of artists, geographical location or historical period. It can be characterised by subject matter – which portrays non-realistic, mystical, mythical or folkloric subjects or events – and style, which is representational and naturalistic, rather than abstract – or in the case of magazine illustrations and similar, in the style of comic art such as manga."

The rise of fantasy and science fiction "pulp" magazines and paperback books in the early part of the 20th century demanded artwork to illustrate stories and (via cover art) to promote sales. There have been many artists that have produced works which fit the definition of fantastic art, and it's therefore impossible to give an exhaustive list of fantastic artists, but major and influential figures include Frank Frazetta, Michael Whelan, Boris Vallejo, John Bauer, Norman Rockwell, Virgil Finlay, Gustavé Doré, and Arthur Rackham.

About the Fantastical Art Gallery

The Fantastical Art Gallery was originally founded in 2011, and quickly garnered an international reputation as one of the most active and productive exponents of the Fantastical and Contemporary Imaginative Realism Art Movement. Maintaining its founding commitment to the promotion and support of artists, as well as forging a recognition for young, emerging, and lesser-known talents, the gallery showcases artists from all over the world, ranging from the emerging, mid-career, and established.

"The Fantastical Art Gallery doesn't exist to categorize or define fantastic art; rather, it exists to honour the imaginations of artists who delight in helping us see the world in a wonderfully different light. It's our job to help them reach a wider appreciative audience!"
